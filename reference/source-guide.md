# Source Guide

## Historical Research
- Google Scholar — peer-reviewed history
- JSTOR — academic journal articles
- Internet Archive — digitized historical newspapers and books
- HathiTrust — historical texts, out-of-copyright books
- Library of Congress Digital Collections
- British Newspaper Archive — UK historical press
- Chronicling America — US historical newspapers

## Medical & Scientific Research
- PubMed — history of medicine
- Wellcome Collection — historical medical archives
- Medscape — practitioner-level, free
- For fiction: a physician beta reader is worth more than any online source

## Legal Research
- Cornell Legal Information Institute — free, authoritative US law
- CourtListener — actual court opinions
- Justia — case law and statutes
- BAILII — British and Irish legal information
- Old Bailey Online — London criminal trials 1674–1913

## Geography & Setting
- Google Street View / Google Earth — current visual reference
- David Rumsey Map Collection — historic maps
- Sanborn Fire Insurance Maps — US historical cities via Library of Congress

## Cultural & Social Research
- Sensitivity readers — no online source substitutes for a human expert
- Own voices nonfiction by members of the community
- Green's Dictionary of Slang — dialect and historical slang
- Oxford English Dictionary — first-use dates of words and phrases

## Red Flags — Flag These to Authors
- Wikipedia as sole source
- AI-generated content cited as fact
- "I've always heard that..." — assumption without verification
- A single forum post or blog
- Outdated editions of reference books
