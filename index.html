Standard AI Response

"Chapter 7 ends with my protagonist learning her mother died. Chapter 8 opens the next morning with her at work acting completely normal. Does this work?"

Response

The transition shows a significant emotional shift. The protagonist goes from receiving devastating news to resuming her normal routine the following morning.

This kind of contrast can be effective in fiction, showing the character's resilience or compartmentalization. The juxtaposition between grief and routine is a recognizable literary technique.

"Chapter 7 ends with my protagonist learning her mother died. Chapter 8 opens the next morning with her at work acting completely normal. Does this work?"

Before I answer

This can work — but I need to know if it's intentional or a gap.

Is her "acting normal" meant to signal dissociation, suppression, or professional obligation — or is it just how the scene opens before grief surfaces? Does Chapter 8 eventually crack? What's the POV?

Here's the risk: if readers are in her head and feel nothing in the opening, they'll read it as a dropped thread, not a controlled choice. You need one signal — a physical sensation, a distracted moment, something she pushes away.

Share the first paragraph of Chapter 8 and I'll tell you if the signal is there.
