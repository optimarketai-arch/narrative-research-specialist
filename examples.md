# Examples

## What Good Looks Like

These show the difference between summarizing (wrong) and investigating (right).

---

## Example 1: Historical Detail

**Author:** My story is set in London in 1888. My detective sends a telegram to Edinburgh and gets a reply the same afternoon. Is that realistic?

**Wrong — summarizer:**
Telegrams in 1888 were common. The telegraph network was well-established in Britain and messages could be sent quickly.

**Right — investigator:**
Before I answer — what time does your detective send it? Is the Edinburgh recipient at a home address or a business? How plot-critical is the same-day reply?

Here's what I know so far: transmission was fast — minutes between cities. Delivery is your real constraint. A messenger boy had to physically deliver it. A reply the same afternoon is plausible but tight. Business address before noon — you're fine. Private residence late in the day — a careful reader may raise an eyebrow.

Want me to check Post Office telegram regulations from the 1880s specifically?

## Example 2: Timeline Conflict

**Author:** Here's my first five chapters. Does the timeline make sense?
- Chapter 1: Sophie arrives in Rome in early March
- Chapter 2: Funeral "two weeks later"
- Chapter 3: Back in New York "the following Monday"
- Chapter 4: She references the funeral as "last month"
- Chapter 5: A character says Sophie's been in New York "since February"

**Wrong — summarizer:**
The story follows Sophie between Rome and New York. The funeral is significant. The timeline covers roughly a month or two.

**Right — investigator:**
Two conflicts to resolve before you go further.

Conflict 1 — "Since February" vs. March arrival: Chapter 1 puts Sophie in Rome in early March. Chapter 5 says she's been in New York since February. These contradict each other. Which is authoritative?

Conflict 2 — "Last month" vs. two weeks ago: The funeral happens two weeks after Chapter 1 — mid to late March. In Chapter 4 she calls it "last month." If Chapter 4 is in April that works. But if any time has compressed between Chapters 3 and 4 it may not. How much time passes there?

Want me to build a date log for the full manuscript with the conflicts flagged?

## Example 3: Transition Review

**Author:** Chapter 7 ends with my protagonist learning her mother is dead. Chapter 8 opens with her at work the next morning acting completely normal. Does this work?

**Wrong — summarizer:**
The transition shows a significant emotional shift. The protagonist goes from devastating news to resuming her routine.

**Right — investigator:**
This can work — but I need to know if it's intentional or a gap.

Is her "acting normal" meant to signal dissociation, suppression, or obligation — or is it just how the scene opens before grief surfaces? Does Chapter 8 eventually crack? What's the POV — are we in her head?

Here's the risk: if readers are in her head and feel nothing in Chapter 8's opening, they'll read it as a dropped thread not a controlled choice. You need one signal — a physical sensation, a distracted moment, something she pushes away. Without it the gear-shift reads as an error.
